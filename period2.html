1607-1757: Colonial North America

From Jamestown to the French and Indian War

Posted by Prithvi Shivaraman on September 25, 2016

An Image of the Era

This image perfectly illustrates the struggle for power between poor white farmers and rich aristocrats. Throughout the colonies, many class conflicts occured before the French and Indian War. Due to indentured servitude, many poor British men came over to the colonies and worked for several years. After they finished their servitude, they did not recieve housing or clothing, and instead went westward to establish their own settlement. One of such settlements, was in Virginia. A white farmer named Nathaniel Bacon lived in Western Virginia, and his community was continually harrased by Native Americans. The current governer of Virginia (Governer Berkeley), did not take any action and this made the poor colonists very angry.

The Burning of Jamestown During Bacon's Rebellion.

Eventually Bacon and his followers decided to take action, and attacked innocent tribes. This actually ended up hurting Governer Berkeley's fur trade operation and he ordered Bacon to stop. Angered with this, Bacon and his followers marched on Jamestown. They ended up burning the entire city to the ground and Berkeley and his forces retreated. Thankfully for Governer Berkeley, Bacon suddenley died due to health reasons, and Berkeley was able to take back the ruined city. This is one of the more famous examples of the early colonial power struggle between different classes and in some ways, caused major changes to ensure that such an event couldn't happen again. In particular, racism was used to united the white colonists against their black slaves.
